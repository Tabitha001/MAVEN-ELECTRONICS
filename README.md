## MAVEN ANALYTICS
This project analyzes sales, customer behavior, product performance, and store operations for Maven Electronics, a multinational electronics retailer. 
Using Power BI, the goal was to uncover patterns in revenue, identify high-performing products and regions, understand customer demographics, and evaluate store performance to support data-driven decision-making.

---

## üéØ Project Overview

The dataset contains several years of transactional sales data, including information about products, customer demographics, geographic regions, sales channels, and store locations.

The project covers four major analytical areas:
1. Executive Overview ‚Äì consolidated KPIs and a high-level summary of revenue, customers, and profitability.
2. Sales Trends ‚Äì year-on-year performance, revenue and profit patterns, weekday ordering behavior.
3. Products & Customers ‚Äì best-selling products, customer segmentation, gender distribution, and product category breakdown.
4. Stores & Regions ‚Äì store performance, revenue distribution by region, and store type comparison.

Each dashboard page was designed to help stakeholders quickly identify business strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities.

## üìà Key Insights

-**Sales Insights**

- Total revenue reached $55.8M, with a 12.1% YoY increase, indicating strong recovery and rising customer demand.
- Customers place the most orders on Thursday and Saturday, with Saturday having the highest order volume.
- Revenue and profit peaked in 2019, followed by a decline beginning in 2020‚Äîlikely tied to market or economic shifts.
- The average unit price remained stable at $356.8, showing consistent pricing across the dataset.


-**Product & Customer Insights**

- The top-selling product generated $505.5K, significantly outperforming the rest of the top 10.
- Loyal customers make up 84.6% of the customer base, indicating strong retention but potential saturation.
- Gender distribution is balanced, with 50.75% female and 49.25% male customers.
- Top revenue-generating product categories include:
Computers ‚Äì $193M
Home Appliances ‚Äì $10.8M
Millennials and Gen Z show increased purchase activity, revealing growing engagement from younger customer groups.

## üí° Recommendations

### 1) Enhance Sales on Peak Days

- Increase marketing efforts on Thursdays and Saturdays, when purchase activity is naturally high.
- Consider targeted promotions, flash sales, and email pushes on these days.

### 2) Strengthen Online Sales Channels
- Online sales account for only 20.45% of revenue, showing room for growth.
- Improve platform experience, reduce checkout friction, and expand online-exclusive offers.

### 3) Improve Performance in Low-Revenue Regions
- Regions like France ($1.2M) require focused attention.
- Use localized marketing, product bundles, and partnerships to boost performance.

### 4) Leverage High Customer Loyalty
- With 84.6% loyal customers, expanding loyalty initiatives can drive retention and spending.
- Add tiered rewards, referral bonuses, and personalized promotions.

### 5) Optimize Inventory for Top Categories
- Ensure proper stock levels for high-performing categories such as Computers and Home Appliances.
- Introduce bundles and dynamic pricing to maximize revenue from top categories.

### 6) Support Low-Performing Products & Stores
- Some products and stores underperform relative to the overall market.
- Use targeted campaigns, bundle offers, and localized strategies to raise performance.
